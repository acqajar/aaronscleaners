Eco-Friendly

Roughly 80% of the nation's 30,000 dry cleaners still employ a cleaning method using the liquid solvent perchloroethylene -- or "perc," according to the New York-based National Cleaners Association. Perc, while known to remove stains and odor effectively without damaging or shrinking delicate garments, has been listed as a hazardous air pollutant by the Clean Air Act and a probable human carcinogen by the International Agency for Research on Cancer. As such, we, at Aaron's Cleaners, are moving to more eco-friendly alternatives, such as liquefied carbon dioxide, silicone and gentle, biodegradable detergents.
